## What is Consonant Gradation?

Consonant gradation (KPT variation) is a sound change where consonants alternate between "strong" and "weak" forms depending on the syllable structure.

## Basic Rules

Consonants change when:
- Adding endings that close/open syllables
- Conjugating verbs
- Declining nouns

## Exceptions

Consonant gradation does NOT apply when:
- **sk, st, tk, hk** - these combinations don't change
- **VV (double vowels)** - Example: vaikea â†’ vaikeaa â†’ vaikean
